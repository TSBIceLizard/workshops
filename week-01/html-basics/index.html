The Cat Mandate of 1965

By decree of the King of cats (including the Parliamentary Ministry of Catkind) of the year 1965 Catizens - whether a house cat, wild cat, Scottish wild cat, or other are hereby expected to execute the following behaviours daily:

  • Meow (ideally a minimum of once every 6 hours)
  • Purr when you are made a fuss of
  • Chase swift dots or small objects
  • Scratch your face on the corners of jagged objects
  • Expect treats
  • Knock over fragile items, whether inside or outside

Should any of these laws be violated, Catizens are expected to receive a good 10 minuites of human fuss and belly rubs.
